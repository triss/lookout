How this works

Count crossings, not people. lookout watches a fixed view, notices movement, and counts each time a moving blob crosses a line you draw. It stores timestamped observations — measurements only — on this device. No faces, no plates, no identity, no cloud, no continuous recording.

This can help estimate movement through a place: people entering or leaving a room, use of a doorway, footfall along a path, bikes passing a point, vehicles using a lane, or activity through a gate. It counts crossings of the line, so treat the result as a practical estimate rather than a perfect head count.

Set the phone somewhere stable with a clear view of the crossing point: a window ledge, shelf, tripod, or another support that will not wobble. Keep the lens clean, avoid reflections where possible, and leave the phone plugged in for longer observations.

  1. Press Start camera and aim at a fixed spot. Line drawing starts automatically.
  2. Use Switch camera for front/back if needed.
  3. Tap two points to place your counting line across the path, doorway, road, or other crossing point.
  4. After the line is positioned, do not move, rotate, or bump the phone. If the view changes, use Redraw line so the line matches the new camera position.
  5. Open Settings to choose direction behaviour and sensitivity.
  6. Once the line is placed, observing starts automatically. The main button becomes Stop observing while counting is active.
  7. Open Share Data to save a CSV/JSON count log, or clear local data.

What is shared. Everything stays on this device. lookout stores measurements only — counts, timestamps and direction — never images or video. Nothing leaves the phone unless you tap Share Data to export a CSV or JSON count log. You can wipe everything at any time with Clear local observations.

Fix common problems in Settings. If leaves, shadows, rain, or small background movement cause false counts, raise Sensitivity threshold or Minimum size. If the same person or vehicle is counted more than once, raise Count cooldown. If quick movement is missed, lower Minimum track duration or increase Target FPS. If the phone is struggling, lower Target FPS or Resolution.

The top bar shows measured fps. If the camera view or phone position changes, stop observing and redraw the line before trusting new counts.
