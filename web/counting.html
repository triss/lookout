How counting works

Count crossings, not people. lookout watches a fixed view, notices movement, and counts each time a moving blob crosses a line you draw. It stores timestamped observations — measurements only — on this device. No faces, no plates, no identity, no cloud, no continuous recording.

  1. Press Start camera and aim at a fixed spot. Line drawing starts automatically.
  2. Use Switch camera for front/back if needed.
  3. Tap two points to place your counting line. Use Redraw line if you need to replace it.
  4. Open Settings to choose direction behaviour, sensitivity, and privacy mode.
  5. Once the line is placed, observing starts automatically. The main button becomes Stop observing while counting is active.
  6. Open Export to save a CSV/JSON count log, or clear local data.

Tune sensitivity, minimum size, minimum duration, and cooldown in Settings to reduce false counts from leaves, shadows, rain, or camera shake. The camera runs at a low frame rate by default to suit older phones; the top bar shows the measured fps.
